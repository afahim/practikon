The following section presents excerpts from a proposal on Edocs, an online environment for collaborating, created by a research team in Qatar. One of the team members believes Edocs facilitates collaboration among professional groups working from different countries. However, their clients (Qatar Foundation researchers) did not find the proposal confident or convincing enough. In the challenges that follow, try to consider the options presented so that the developers can make the most effective case for their audience.

Directions:
Identify and improve text that can make the report more effective, in terms of its metadiscourse, nominalizations, and concision.
Focus: Metadiscourse

Challenge 1: Using Metadiscourse

Your Goal:

Use intensifiers and/or remove hedgers to make the tone more confident

Using Intensifiers or Remove Hedgers

1. Describing negative situations where your purpose is not to protect the person or thing associated with the negativity

More Protection:

The guides seem to have too much information to fit on a mobile screen.

Less Protection:

The guides have too much information to fit on a mobile screen.

2. Being certain or direct or showing emphasis

Less Certain:

This activity may be able to help you use metadiscourse in your next paper.

More Certain:

This activity will help you use metadiscourse in your next paper.

Less Emphasis:

I went through the activities.

More Emphasis:

I went through all of the activities.

Feedback on Response Options

Option A

Teams typically

This option removes a couple hedgers (“some”, “sometimes”) to add more certainty and place emphasis on the the team’s actions. This would be appropriate if your purpose is to emphasize these actions.

Option B

Some teams

This option keeps a hedger (“some”) which places less emphasis on the team’s actions. This would be appropriate if your purpose is to place less emphasis on the actions.

Option C

It is possible that teams

This option uses a hedger (“possible”) which places even less emphasis on the team’s actions. This would be appropriate for a different type of document where you are not making an argument.
